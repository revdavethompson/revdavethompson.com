A CIVIL HUMAN Ethic

For over twenty years, Rev. Dave has worked to build common ground and peace within our most divided of organizations, faith groups, and politics. His long work and experience gave him the opportunity to discover best-practices that moved people more quickly to common ground, and helped to maintain that peace.

As a CIVIL HUMAN Servant, Rev. Dave now shares his CIVIL HUMAN Ethic; teaching people, businessess and organizations the best practices for overcoming division, building common gournd, and establishing a peaceful culture.

H.U.M.A.N. Standards

These five standards represent the most basic and necessary criteria for creating human common ground.

  • H

    Honest

    We must be honest about what we can and cannot observe or measure.

  • U

    Understandable

    We must work to build understanding, so that every person can truly understand what is being agreed upon.

  • M

    Measurable

    We must provide measurable proof for the claims that we make and accept.

  • A

    Achievable

    We must only require of others what is achievable without suffering.

  • N

    Negotiable

    In an ever-changing universe, we must agree to be negotiable with our rules, so they continue to remain CIVIL and HUMAN.

C.I.V.I.L. Freedoms

These pillars represent our most fundamental and divisive of human needs. Every person must be free to disagree on these matters if we wish to maintain our common ground and peace.

  • C

    Creed

    We must respect every person's freedom to form their own creed conclusions about what we cannot observe.

  • I

    Independence

    We must respect every person's freedom to be independent from the control of others.

  • V

    Vote

    We must respect every person's freedom to vote and choose those who will have control over them.

  • I

    Intimacy

    We must respect every person's freedom to intimately love the person of their own choosing.

  • L

    Lifestyle

    We must respect every person's freedom to live, and to pursue a lifestyle that makes them happy.
