A CIVIL HUMAN Minister

I have dedicated my life to serving humanity in those principles that we can all hold in common.

Though we are divided by our nations, our faiths, and our politics, together we can find common ground by agreeing to the most basic of CIVIL HUMAN Ethics.

CIVIL

If we desire to live in peace with one another, it is necessary that we allow each other certain basic rights:

  • (C)reed. We all need to be free to draw our own conclusions about the unprovable.
  • (I)ndependence. We all need to be free from the control of others.
  • (V)ote. We all need to choose those who will govern us.
  • (I)ntimacy. We all need to be able to intimately love whomever we choose.
  • (L)ife. We all need the ability to live, and without cure, to die.

HUMAN

After working across religious and political divides for nearly two decades I discovered that common ground can only be found when we strive to make our communication:

  • (H)onest. We must be honest about what we observe and what we cannot observe.
  • (U)nderstandable. We must communicate so that all people can understand.
  • (M)easurable. We must provide measurable proof for the claims we make.
  • (A)chievable. We must only expect of others what they can achieve without suffering.
  • (N)egotiable. We must always negotiate our rules so that they remain HUMAN in a constantly changing universe.

Minister

I am a licensed and ordained minister. I serve our human community though the following core values:

  • Political & Neutrality. I will never publicly endorse or criticize any political candidate, organization, or party.
  • Education. Every human begins as clean slate. I believe it is critical that we educate every person in the basic forms of respect, rights and our mutual expectations of human interaction.
  • Confession. As humans we find healing from trauma by sharing and reliving these traumas in the safe and comforting moments of confession to one another.
  • Empathy. Through interviews and the creative arts, I work to foster empathy by giving people the ability to share their human stories and experiences.

About Me

A few highlights of my work & ministry.

  • 1995

    Ministry Education

    Northwest University

    I began my ministerial journey and studies with a BA in Religion & Philosophy, minoring in Biblical languages, while also serving as an associate minister in a church in Corvallis, OR.

  • 1997

    Theology Education

    Gordon-Conwell Theological Seminary

    I continued my professional studies, entering a Masters in Theology program, forcusing on conflict resolution. Additionally, I received a Certificate in Religion & Social Conflict from the Boston Theological Institute, while also working in distributed and online education.

  • 2000

    Education Administration

    Northwest University

    Rev. Thompson works in Education Administration, focusing on non-traditional students and serving as the Director of Online Education.

  • 2003

    Political Consulting

    Policorp Corporation

    In an effort to better understand social conflict, I launched a political consulting firm and was fortunate to work with local, state and US congressional campaigns, managing communications and public relations. I also built and launched one of the first open source software platforms to manage political campaigns.

  • 2007

    Fortune 500 & Tech

    Starbucks Corporation

    While closing my political consulting firm, I worked as a Barista. I then was very fortunate to receive the largest promotion in Starbucks history, becoming the Lead Application Developer for Starbucks' Global Business Services Solutions.

  • 2009

    Recorded Musician

    Disclaimer CD

    I have spent most of my life performing music (piano/singing) in venues of all sorts. In 2009 I decided to check a box off my bucket list, and put out a cd of my own original compositions called Disclaimer. I was fortunate to secure a great producer, an amazing studio band and an exceptional recording studio/engineer.

  • 2010

    Author & Speaker

    Over Coffee

    After years of reseach and reflection, I returned to ministry and launched my first book, Over Coffee: a conversation for gay partnership and conservative faith. I decided to reach across the political and spiritual divide to meet privately, over coffee, with hundreds of conservative ministers and leaders, providing a safe-space for them to talk about gay (and other sexuality) issues in their ministries and organizations.

  • 2015

    YouTube Show Host

    Over Coffee with Rev. Dave Thompson

    I launched a youtube channel where I interview weekly guests about their spiritual story.
