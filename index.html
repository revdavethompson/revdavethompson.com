C.I.V.I.L. Needs

I have found that peace begins by respecting these most basic human needs regardless of our political, spiritual and cultural divides.

  • (C)reed. We must respect each other's need to draw our own conclusions about the unprovable.
  • (I)ndependence. We must respect each other's need to be independent from the control of others.
  • (V)ote. We must respect each other's need to choose those who will govern us.
  • (I)ntimacy. We must respect each other's need to intimately love whomever we choose.
  • (L)ife. We must respect each other's need to live, and if we are without cure, to die.

H.U.M.A.N. Interaction

  • (H)onesty. We must be honest with each other about what we observe and what we cannot observe.(Translation and interpretation)
  • (U)nderstandable. We must communicate with each other in a way that can be understood by each another. (Defining Gay. (John the straight gay guy.)
  • (M)easurable. We must provide measurable proof for the claims we make. (Conversion Therapy)
  • (A)chievable. We must only ask of others what they can achieve without suffering. (Celibacy)
  • (N)egotiable. We must always negotiate our rules so that they remain HUMAN in a universe that changes unceasingly. (New Wineskins for new wine)

Minister
Core Values

In order to serve our common human community, I have committed to the following Core Values.

  • Political & Religious Neutrality
    So that I may speak to all humans, without prejudice, I will never publicly endorse or criticize any political or religious person or organization.
  • CIVIL HUMAN Education
    Every human begins as a clean slate. I believe it is therefore critical that we educate each other about what we should respect about each other, and how we should interact with each other.
  • Confession
    Chaos and Conflict will always exist in our ever changing universe. As humans we find healing from these traumas by sharing and re-living these traumas in the safe and comforting context of confession with one-another.
  • Empathy
    Our greatest obstacle to peace is the illusion that we are more different than we are similar. In fact, the opposite is true. Through interviews and the creative arts, I work to foster empathy by giving people the ability to relate their human stories and experiences.

Over Coffee

Over Coffee is a practice of empathy.

I have discovered that the most important first step to finding peace and common ground is to understand each other's human story.

I began having coffee, one-on-one, with ministers and leaders across the political, spiritual and cultural divide. Rather than beginning by listening to our differences, I began by listening to our similarities. This practice of empathy, not only allowed us to see each other as human, but it allowed us to better understand the reason why we see things differently.

I decided to take the practice from meeting in private, to meeting in public so that other people could see a practical example of how to listen and learn from each other.

The Over Coffee Program is a practical way for you to engage your congregation, organization, or classroom in the practice of empathy.

  • This stage-based ministry is a practice of empathy, an opportunity for your congregation to get to know each other and to develop an understanding of their similarities, rather than their differences.
  • I will ask you to choose up to three individuals from your congregation to join me on stage, one at a time, at a table over coffee. There I will interview them with a few questions about their human story, focusing on aspects of common human experience. (E.g. “What was your favorite memory of your mother/father?”)
  • My questions will focus on stories that can help congregation members find common ground and experiences.

Confession

A Safe Space for Sharing Your Burdens.

It has been my utmost privilege to meet privately with hundreds of ministers such as yourself, across the spiritual spectrum. In that space of safety and acceptance, I have listened to their very real and very human confessions. For many, simply having a place where they can talk openly about the struggles they are facing, whether personally, with their congregation, or leadership, has provided immeasurable relief.

  • I would be honored to help shoulder your burdens and would welcome the opportunity to meet with you in order to listen, without record, without judgment and without consequence.
  • I would also welcome the opportunity to offer your congregation the same opportunity. It is my practice to set aside time after each service where people can meet with me and offer their confessions and to share a burden they are carrying.
