A CIVIL HUMAN

We are living in an internet world where our once clear notions of borders, nations and cultures have begun to bleed over into each other.

There has never been a more important time for us as humans to ask a simple but necessary question. Will we continue to fight over who's culture will rule supreme, or can we at least agree to some of the most basic rules of living that will allow us to find the common ground necessary to create a lasting peace?

Rev. Dave Thompson has spent over twenty years building common ground in our most divided of social groups and issues. His long experience of creating peace provided him the opportunity to learn some of the best practices and to developed his CIVIL HUMAN Ethic for finding the common ground that creates peace.

As a CIVIL HUMAN Servant, Rev Dave has chosen to focus his work on those things that every human being can hold in common. As such, he upholds the ideal that every human, regardless of race, creed, or color, should at very least respect our C.I.V.I.L. freedoms and uphold our H.U.M.A.N. ethics when deciding on the rules we agree to follow as a society.

H.U.M.A.N. Standards

These five standards represent the most basic and necessary criteria for creating human common ground.

  • H

    Honest

    We must be honest about what we can and cannot observe or measure.

  • U

    Understandable

    We must work to build understanding, so that every person can truly understand what is being agreed upon.

  • M

    Measurable

    We must provide measurable proof for the claims that we make and accept.

  • A

    Achievable

    We must only require of others what is achievable without suffering.

  • N

    Negotiable

    In an ever-changing universe, we must agree to be negotiable with our rules, so they continue to remain CIVIL and HUMAN.

C.I.V.I.L. Freedoms

These pillars represent our most fundamental and divisive of human needs. Every person must be free to disagree on these matters if we wish to maintain our common ground and peace.

  • C

    Creed

    We must respect every person's freedom to form their own creed conclusions about what we cannot observe.

  • I

    Independence

    We must respect every person's freedom to be independent from the control of others.

  • V

    Vote

    We must respect every person's freedom to vote and choose those who will have control over them.

  • I

    Intimacy

    We must respect every person's freedom to intimately love the person of their own choosing.

  • L

    Lifestyle

    We must respect every person's freedom to live, and to pursue a lifestyle that makes them happy.
